Why BidFrame

Production bidding is still trapped in spreadsheets, PDFs, and gut feel.

Every producer has built a bid late at night from a blank template, an old job, and a pile of assumptions.

BidFrame gets the first pass on the page so you can spend your time checking the decisions that matter.

The workflow

Start with the brief. Finish with your bid.

BidFrame handles the first-pass structure and repetitive setup. You check the scope, rates, and assumptions before anything is applied.

01

Upload the brief

Use PDF, Word, PowerPoint, text, Markdown, or paste the brief directly. No brief yet? Answer a short intake about the job instead, or start from a template for your production type.

02

Verify the scope

Review extracted deliverables, locations, usage, schedule, budget signals, and open questions.

03

Review the first pass

Select the lines you want and adjust quantities and rates. After Apply, every line remains editable in the bid builder.

04

Resolve and export

Work through budget and risk tasks, save a version, then deliver the format the job requires.

What the review surfaces

The misses that cost real money.

A bid can look complete and still leave the production company exposed. BidFrame puts the assumptions most likely to affect scope, margin, and cash flow in front of you before the bid goes out.

01

Rights that are not fully scoped.

Talent term, territory, platforms, music sync and master use, and renewal exposure stay open until you answer them or add a producer note.

02

Union math the schedule hides.

SAG-AFTRA P&H can add 23.5% to covered talent compensation. On $150,000 of covered compensation, that is $35,250 that must be accounted for. Rates below a sourced floor and long days without premium coverage are flagged for review.

Questions producers ask

What BidFrame does, and where it stops.

Clear answers before you bring a live brief into the workspace.

Does BidFrame replace the producer?

No. It prepares the first pass, calculates totals, and organizes the open decisions. The producer checks the scope, changes the numbers, and approves the bid.

What if I don't have a brief?

You don't need one. Describe the job in the structured intake: deliverables, schedule, locations, and usage. BidFrame drafts from that. Or open a starter template for your production type and build the bid directly. Intake answers are kept separate from parsed brief facts, so nothing you typed gets treated as something the client said.

Can I use my company rate card?

Yes. Exact role matches from the active company rate card take priority. BidFrame baselines can fill uncovered roles, and estimates or quote-required costs remain labeled.

Does it support union and non-union bids?

BidFrame supports union, non-union, and mixed productions. Published scales, sourced floors, jurisdiction, and organization settings inform the draft. The producer confirms the agreement and final rates.

What can I send to a client?

Client-facing HTML and PDF bids, AICP-oriented HTML, PDF, and XLSX bid forms, and one-page bid letters in HTML or PDF. Internal CSV and XLSX exports keep working detail separate.

Does it replace production management after award?

No. BidFrame is pre-award intelligence. Your existing tools take over after award for production management, actuals, timesheets, invoicing, and client approvals.
